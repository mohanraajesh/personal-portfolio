A Digital Age Revelation

Based on a true event

We think being online means we're present. But one silent exit changed my perception.

It was just a WhatsApp group. Quiet. Casual.

Then one day… someone left. No message. No goodbye.

A month later, we found out—that person had a heart attack and passed away.

A few days later: No LinkedIn profile, no social media posts, no digital trace and an exit from WhatsApp group. Gone. Completely.

In this digital age, absence can be absolute.

Presence isn’t a profile. It’s a connection.

And silence doesn’t mean nothing.
It might mean… everything.

"Are we connected to mere profiles, or is it about genuine connection?"
